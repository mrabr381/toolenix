What is Return on Investment (ROI)?

Whether you are investing in real estate, launching a digital marketing campaign, or buying stocks, every financial decision boils down to one fundamental question: "Was it worth the cost?"

Return on Investment (ROI) is a universal financial metric used to evaluate the efficiency and profitability of an investment. It measures the amount of return (net profit) on a particular asset relative to the investment's original cost. Expressed as a percentage, a high ROI means the investment's gains compare favorably to its cost, while a negative ROI indicates a financial loss.

How to Calculate ROI

The standard algebraic formula for calculating Return on Investment is relatively straightforward:

ROI = [(Amount Returned - Amount Invested) / Amount Invested] × 100

Practical Example: Suppose you spend $500 running Facebook Ads for an e-book. Because of those ads, you generate $1,500 in total book sales.
1. Net Profit = $1,500 - $500 = $1,000.
2. ROI = ($1,000 / $500) × 100 = 200%.

This means for every $1 you spent on advertising, you received your $1 back, plus an additional $2 in pure profit.

The Importance of Annualized ROI

While standard ROI is a great metric, it has one major flaw: It ignores the passage of time.

Imagine Investment A yields a 50% ROI, and Investment B yields a 30% ROI. At first glance, Investment A looks better. However, what if Investment A took 5 years to achieve that 50%, while Investment B took only 1 year to achieve 30%?

To accurately compare investments over different timeframes, financial analysts use Annualized ROI. This calculation smooths out the return, showing you the equivalent average growth rate per year.
