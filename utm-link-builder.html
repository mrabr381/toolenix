What Are UTM Parameters?

UTM parameters are short text codes that you append to the end of your standard website URL. They do not change the destination or the content of the page; they simply whisper hidden data to your analytics software when a user clicks the link.

A properly structured UTM link contains a question mark ? to start the query string, followed by key-value pairs separated by ampersands &. For example: ?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=social.

Understanding the Core UTM Tags

To use this tool effectively, you must understand the hierarchy of the five standard parameters. Consistency in naming conventions is the key to clean analytics data.

1. Campaign Source (utm_source)

This tells Analytics where the traffic came from. It is the specific platform, vendor, or application. Examples include: google, facebook, mailchimp, tiktok.

2. Campaign Medium (utm_medium)

This tells Analytics how the traffic got to you. It describes the broader marketing channel. Examples include: cpc (cost-per-click/paid ads), social (organic social media), email, affiliate.

3. Campaign Name (utm_campaign)

This identifies the specific promotion, product launch, or strategic initiative you are running. Examples include: spring_sale_2026, ebook_launch, retargeting_campaign.

4. Campaign Term (utm_term)

This is an optional parameter primarily used for Paid Search (PPC). It identifies the exact keyword that triggered your ad. Example: buy_running_shoes.

5. Campaign Content (utm_content)

This optional parameter is brilliant for A/B testing. If you have two different links pointing to the same URL in a single email (e.g., a blue button and a text link), you use this tag to differentiate them. Examples: blue_cta_button, footer_text_link.

Why Encoding Matters

A common mistake marketers make when manually typing UTM links is including spaces or special characters. If your campaign name is "Spring Sale!", typing ?utm_campaign=Spring Sale! will break the URL structure in many browsers. Our tool utilizes robust client-side JavaScript to automatically apply Percent-Encoding (e.g., converting spaces to %20), ensuring your tracking links work flawlessly on every device.
