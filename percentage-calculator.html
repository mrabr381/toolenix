Understanding the Mathematics of Percentages

The word "percentage" comes from the Latin phrase per centum, which literally translates to "by the hundred." In mathematics, a percentage is simply a ratio or a number expressed as a fraction of 100. It is the universal standard for expressing proportions, making it incredibly easy to compare two vastly different quantities.

Whether you are calculating the tip on a restaurant bill, determining the sales tax on a new computer, or evaluating the profit margin on your dropshipping store, percentage calculations are an unavoidable part of daily life. The Three Core Percentage Formulas

1. What is X% of Y? (The Basic Percentage)

This is the most common calculation. You use this when you know the total amount and the percentage, and you need to find the numerical value.
Formula: $Value = (\frac{X}{100}) \times Y$
Example: You are buying a $1,200 laptop and the sales tax is 8%. What is 8% of $1,200? The answer is $96.

2. X is what percent of Y? (The Ratio)

You use this calculation to find the percentage when you already have the two numbers. This is exactly how teachers calculate test scores.
Formula: $Percentage = (\frac{X}{Y}) \times 100$
Example: You scored 45 out of 60 on a math exam. 45 is what percent of 60? The answer is 75%.

3. Percentage Increase or Decrease

This formula tracks change over time. It is heavily utilized in finance, stock market trading, and retail pricing. It tells you exactly how much a value has grown or shrunk relative to its original size.
Formula: $Change = (\frac{Y - X}{|X|}) \times 100$
Example: Your rent was $1,000 last year, and now it is $1,150. What is the percentage increase? The answer is a 15% increase.
